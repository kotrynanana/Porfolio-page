Petmade

Rebranding

- 2023 -

About the project

Petmade, formerly known as Pawmade, is a dog supplement brand that needed a refreshed identity to reflect its new name and stand out in a competitive market. The goal was to create a modern and minimal design that would differentiate the brand on store shelves while maintaining the trust and credibility associated with medical-grade supplements.

Through extensive research and careful design decisions, the new brand identity strikes a balance between vibrant, eye-catching aesthetics and a professional, health-focused tone.

Design Process

  • Market Research

    Conducted an in-depth analysis of competitor brands in the dog supplement industry to identify trends and gaps, ensuring Petmade's new identity would stand out.

  • Brand Strategy

    Developed a visual identity that balances a minimal and colorful design with a medical feel, emphasizing that Petmade products are supplements rather than treats.

  • Logo Redesign

    Updated the logo by incorporating a paw symbol in place of the last “a” in Petmade, creating a simple and recognizable mark that subtly reinforces the brand's focus on pets, while keeping the overall design clean and approachable

  • Packaging Design

    Designed packaging that is vibrant and minimal, making it appealing and easy to spot on store shelves, while maintaining a professional, supplement-focused aesthetic.

    More about Packaging design

  • Unique Illustrations

    Created custom illustrations of dogs to further enhance the brand’s personality and connect with pet owners. These illustrations add a playful touch while reinforcing the brand's focus on pets.

  • Color Palette & Typography

    Selected a blue and yellow color scheme, the two colors dogs can see best, to create packaging that may also appeal to them. This strategic choice not only enhances shelf visibility but also opens possibilities for future product extensions, like branded toys that dogs would enjoy. The typography was chosen to balance professionalism and approachability, aligning with the brand's identity as a supplement provider.

  • Feedback & Refinement

    Iterated on designs based on client feedback, ensuring that the final brand identity met both aesthetic and functional goals for Petmade.x

Research Overview

I conducted an in-depth analysis of around 100 pet supplement brands in the US, focusing on the most successful ones. I grouped these brands based on their approach and selling channels and noticed a clear trend: top brands like ZestyPaws or PetLab had recently rebranded, adopting bold, minimal designs.

As Petmade was already undergoing a name change, it was the perfect opportunity to update all visuals. My research showed that keeping up with these design trends would make Petmade more competitive and noticeable both online and in stores. However, I proposed a more subtle approach, balancing boldness with a style that would resonate with our unique target audience.
